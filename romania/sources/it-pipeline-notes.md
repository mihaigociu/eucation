## 1. Why this matters for the brief

QX and OECD focus on the failure side. Neither names UiPath, Bitdefender, the
big-tech R&D footprint, the Olympiad infrastructure, or the *colegii naționale*
that feed it. Yet the paradox the brief is built around — globally competitive
STEM elite alongside mass functional illiteracy — *requires* this side of the
ledger.

The single sharpest framing this material supports: **Romania has a small,
high-performing IT elite that contributes disproportionately to GDP but does
not reach broad employment.** This is consistent with — not contradicted by —
the failure-side data. The system is producing two outcomes simultaneously.

## 3. The workforce paradox — large sector, narrow labour share

Romania's IT sector is large in *absolute* terms but ICT specialists are a
small *share* of total employment. This is the single most important framing
nuance.

| Country | ICT specialists as % of total employment (2024) |
|---|---|
| Sweden | 8.6% |
| Luxembourg | 8.0% |
| EU average | **5.0%** |
| Romania | **2.8%** |
| Greece | 2.5% |

Romania ranks **second-lowest in the EU** on this measure — alongside Greece —
despite hosting one of the EU's highest IT-sector GDP shares. This is a
narrow elite, not a broad-based digital economy.

**Other Eurostat 2024 features for Romania:**
- 2nd-highest share of women among ICT specialists (27.3%) in the EU, after Estonia.
- Aging workforce: +13.4 pp shift toward older specialists (35-74) since 2014.
- Rising tertiary attainment among ICT specialists (top-5 EU rise since 2014).

> Source: [Eurostat news, July 2025](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/web/products-eurostat-news/w/ddn-20250708-2);
> [Eurostat Statistics Explained, ICT specialists in employment](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/statistics-explained/index.php?title=ICT_specialists_in_employment).

## 4. The Olympiad pipeline — direct evidence of the elite track

### 4.1 International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO)

- **Romania hosted the first IMO in 1959** and won.
- All-time medal count: **45 gold, 85 silver, 65 bronze** (195 total).
- Average ranking: **7.5**.
- Consistent top-10 presence for 60+ years.
- IMO 2022: **1st in Europe, 5th worldwide** — "best performance in the last 25 years" (Romania Insider).

> Sources: [IMO official Romania page](https://www.imo-official.org/country_individual_r.aspx?code=ROU);
> [Wikipedia: List of countries by IMO medal count](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_medal_count_at_International_Mathematical_Olympiad);
> [Romania Insider, IMO 2022](https://www.romania-insider.com/romania-international-math-olympiad-2022).

### 4.2 International Olympiad in Informatics (IOI)

- First participation 1990. **36 years**, 95 contestants total.
- All-time medal count: **38 gold, 60 silver, 37 bronze** (135 total).
- Ranked **#6 worldwide** as of 2026 by total medals.
- Zero honourable mentions — every Romanian who finishes wins a medal.

> Source: [IOI official statistics, Romania](https://stats.ioinformatics.org/countries/ROU).

### 4.3 What the Olympiad data implies for the brief

These numbers carry the elite-track half of the paradox by themselves.
Romania's IMO performance places it among countries with vastly more
population and resources (it sits in the company of US, China, Russia,
South Korea, Hungary). The infrastructure that produces this — *centre de
excelență*, specific *colegii naționale*, a small network of teachers and
coaches — is the operational base for the success-side narrative.

**Gap to fill:** which schools the medallists come from. Edupedu /
Olimpiada.info publishes school-level data on national stages, but
international-team school distribution is harder to pull. Worth a focused
research pass.

## 5. Big-tech footprint and homegrown unicorns

### 5.1 Multinational R&D centres (selective)

- **Microsoft Bucharest** — 1,500+ engineers, Azure / Office 365 / Dynamics.
  Plus business support centres in București and Timișoara.
- **Amazon** — engineering and AWS operations in Bucharest and Iași.
- **Google, Oracle, Dell, Adobe, IBM, fitbit, Redis** — all with Bucharest
  engineering operations.
- **Continental, Bosch** — automotive R&D, mostly Cluj and Timișoara
  (consequential for the engineering-graduate sink).

### 5.2 Homegrown unicorns

| Company | Founded | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **UiPath** | Bucharest, 2005 | RPA platform; went public 2021 at ~$35B valuation; R&D still substantially in Romania. Iconic Romanian tech success story. |
| **Bitdefender** | Bucharest, 2001 | Cybersecurity; ~500M users globally; HQ + main R&D in Romania. |
| **eMAG** | Bucharest, 2001 | E-commerce; dominant in RO + expanding in CEE. |

Also notable but not (yet) unicorn-scale: FintechOS, TypingDNA, Druid AI,
DRUID, TotalSoft. The Cluj cluster has its own scene (8th Floor, Tapptitude,
etc.).

### 5.3 Geographic concentration

- **București** ≈ **63%** of software-development revenues.
- Secondary hubs: Cluj-Napoca, Iași, Timișoara, Craiova.
- Cluj benefits visibly from UBB + UTCN graduate flow; Iași from UAIC; Timișoara from UPT.
- This mirrors the broader "two Romanias" thesis in the failure-side data:
  the same urban concentration that produces high tertiary attainment also
  produces tech employment.

> Sources: [The Recursive — Mapping Romania's innovation cities](https://therecursive.com/mapping-romanian-cities-that-are-shapping-tech-innovation/);
> [N-iX — Software development in Romania](https://www.n-ix.com/software-development-romania-market-overview/).

## 6. Connecting the two halves of the paradox

The brief should resist framing the IT success as "Romania's compensation"
for the school system's failure. The two are not in tension — they coexist
in the same equilibrium:

1. **A small elite track** runs from top *colegii naționale* → math/informatics
   Olympiad infrastructure → UPB / UTCN / UBB / UAIC computer science +
   automatică → multinational R&D centre or unicorn → senior role or emigration.
2. **A separate mass track** runs from rural and disadvantaged urban schools →
   functional illiteracy by liceu → dropout or low-skill labour market entry.
3. The mass track does not feed the elite track. The elite track does not
   pull the mass track up. They are two parallel systems sharing physical
   buildings and a notional curriculum.

Evidence that supports this dual-system reading, already in our sources:

- **OECD 2025:** "While a minority of Romanian students reach levels of
  excellence comparable to their peers in other European and OECD countries,
  many more leave school without mastering the basic competencies."
- **OECD 2025:** Largest rural-urban PISA gap of any participating country,
  *driven by SES*. The two tracks are stratified by where you live and what
  your parents do.
- **BRIO 2025:** Functional numeric illiteracy in urban-mare = 15% at primar,
  30% at liceu. In rural = 43% → 73%. Two completely different educational
  realities.
- **Eurostat 2024:** ICT specialists 2.8% of total employment — second-lowest
  in EU. The elite track is narrow; it does not absorb the population.
- **OECD 2025:** Tertiary attainment 19% (EU 41%). The pipeline outputs are
  small relative to peers, even though they're high-quality at the top end.

**The brief's central claim, sharpened:** Romania's education system is not
"failing on average" — it is producing two outputs at once: a small,
internationally competitive technical elite and a large cohort of
functionally illiterate dropouts. The reforms in flight (QX, the 2023 laws,
PNRR) are attempts to broaden the base without disturbing the elite. Whether
that's politically feasible is the open question.

## 7. Caveats and gaps

- **Headcount triangulation:** ANIS says 195,929 IT employees; Eurostat says
  207,800 ICT specialists; industry sources say ~212,000 developers. Different
  methodologies. Pin one definition before citing.
- **GDP contribution numbers** include indirect/induced multipliers; the
  direct-only 6.67% is the safer figure for headline use.
- **Brain drain data is missing.** Anecdotal: large share of senior engineers
  in Romanian R&D centres at FAANG / unicorns abroad are diaspora. Quantifying
  this needs OECD or EC mobility statistics.
- **Olympiad → workforce conversion rate is missing.** Of the ~10 Romanian
  IMO/IOI medallists per year, how many stay vs. emigrate? Likely most
  emigrate (CMU, MIT, ETH Zurich, EPFL). This deserves a dedicated lookup.
- **Salary data and wage premium for IT vs. national median** — Brainspotting,
  eJobs, BestJobs publish annual reports. Worth pulling.
- **Tax-incentive politics:** the elimination of the IT income-tax exemption
  in 2023-2024 is a live political fight. The brief should cover it in §5.8
  (reforms) without taking a position.
