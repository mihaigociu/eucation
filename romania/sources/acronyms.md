# Acronyms and Glossary

> Quick reference for the abbreviations that appear in this project's
> source materials and brief drafts. When a Romanian and an
> English/OECD term refer to the same thing, both are given. Where the
> mapping is not exact, the asymmetry is noted.
>
> The OECD 2025 *Education and Skills in Romania* review also has its
> own acronyms front-matter (PDF p.12-13) — that is the authoritative
> list for OECD-specific usage.

---

## A

**A doua șansă** — "Second Chance". National re-entry programme for adults
without compulsory-schooling completion. Run by the Ministry of Education
in cooperation with NGOs.

**ANC** — *Autoritatea Națională pentru Calificări* / National
Qualification Authority. Sits within the Ministry of Education and is
the EU-aligned competent body for occupational and educational
qualifications.

**ANIS** — *Asociația Patronală a Industriei de Software și Servicii* /
the Romanian Software & IT Services Industry Association. The primary
industry body publishing aggregate data on the Romanian IT sector.

**ANOFM** — *Agenția Națională pentru Ocuparea Forței de Muncă* / National
Employment Agency. The Romanian public employment service (the OECD
calls it the **PES**, Public Employment Service).

**ARACIP / ARACIIP** — *Agenția Română de Asigurare a Calității în
Învățământul Preuniversitar* / Romanian Agency for Quality Assurance and
Inspection in Pre-University Education. Renamed ARACIIP (adding
*Inspecție* / "Inspection") under the 2023 Law 198. Both names appear in
documents from 2023-2025.

**ARACIS** — *Agenția Română de Asigurare a Calității în Învățământul
Superior* / Romanian Agency for Quality Assurance in Higher Education.
The HE equivalent of ARACIP.

## B

**Bacalaureatul / "Bac"** — The Romanian school-leaving and university-
entry examination at the end of grade 12. Around 70-75% pass rate
nationally, with sharp variation by track (theoretical ~88%, vocational
~46% per OECD 2018-2019 data).

**BRIO** — Romanian commercial educational-assessment platform; produces
standardised normative testing of school competencies. Authors of the
2025 numeric-literacy report cited throughout.

## C

**CDI** — *Cercetare-Dezvoltare-Inovare* / Research, Development &
Innovation (R&D&I). The QX report's first chapter is on the Romanian CDI
system. OECD uses **R&D** or **R&I** for the same thing.

**CEAE** — *Centrul de Evaluare și Analize Educaționale* / Centre for
Educational Evaluation and Analysis. Independent Romanian education
think tank.

**CEE** — Central and Eastern Europe. OECD's standard regional grouping
that includes Romania, Bulgaria, Czechia, Hungary, Poland, Slovakia, the
Baltics, and Slovenia.

**CES** — *Cerințe Educaționale Speciale* / Special Educational Needs
(English **SEN**). The same population, different name.

**CJRAE** — *Centrul Județean de Resurse și Asistență Educațională* /
County Centre for Educational Resources and Assistance. The county-level
specialist-support unit (counsellors, speech therapists, school
psychologists).

**CNFIS** — National Commission for the Financing of Higher Education.
Sets the funding-allocation methodology for state universities.

**Colegii naționale** — National Colleges. A formal status awarded to
the highest-performing pre-university institutions; in practice a small
set of urban high schools that concentrate Olympiad medallists. See §5.4.

**CRED** — *Curriculum Relevant, Educație Deschisă* / Relevant Curriculum,
Open Education for All. EU-funded curriculum-modernisation project
2017-2023.

**CSI** — *Inspectorat Școlar Județean* / County School Inspectorate. The
county-level body that supervises pre-university schools. Being
restructured into **County Directorates for Pre-University Education**
under the 2023 law.

**CVET** — Continuing Vocational Education and Training. Training for
adults already in the workforce.

## D

**Dual VET** — A vocational training model combining classroom
instruction with structured work-based learning at a partner employer.
Standard in Austria and Germany. Romania's PNRR includes 29 regional
dual VET campuses in development. See §5.5.

## E

**ECEC** — Early Childhood Education and Care. OECD term for the combined
*antepreșcolar* (0-3) and *preșcolar* (3-6) levels.

**Edupedu.ro** — Single best Romanian-language journalism on education
policy and data. Publishes annual Bac and Evaluare Națională school-level
results.

**EFOR** — *Expert Forum*. Romanian governance and policy think tank.
Monitors PNRR implementation.

**Eurostat** — The EU statistical office. Source for the early-leaver,
ICT-specialist, and education-spending comparator data used throughout.

**Evaluare Națională** — National Evaluation. High-stakes exam at the end
of *gimnaziu* (grade 8). The score determines high-school admission.

## I

**IMO** — International Mathematical Olympiad. Annual since 1959 (Romania
hosted the first). Romania ranks #6 worldwide all-time by gold medals,
5th per-capita. See §5.4 and chart 05.

**INCD** — *Institut Național de Cercetare-Dezvoltare* / National R&D
Institute. The 43 INCDs are the operational core of the public-sector R&D
system; together with universities, the Romanian Academy units, and
private R&D, they comprise the national CDI system.

**INS** — *Institutul Național de Statistică* / National Institute of
Statistics. The Romanian statistical office. Primary source for the
*Tempo Online* database.

**IOI** — International Olympiad in Informatics. Annual since 1989;
Romania first competed in 1990 and ranks #6 worldwide by total medals.
See §5.4 and chart 05.

**ISCED** — International Standard Classification of Education. UNESCO's
level taxonomy: ISCED 1 = primary, 2 = lower secondary, 3 = upper
secondary, 4 = post-secondary non-tertiary, 5-8 = tertiary
(short-cycle / bachelor's / master's / doctoral).

**IVET** — Initial Vocational Education and Training. The school-based
vocational track for students still in compulsory schooling, as opposed
to **CVET** for adults.

## L

**LLL** — Lifelong Learning / *învățarea (educația/formarea) pe tot
parcursul vieții*. Adult education and training across the working life.
Romania's adult participation in LLL is 5.4% (2022) vs an EU average of
11.9% — among the EU's lowest. See QX §4 and OECD 2025 ch. 6.

## M

**MATE** — Early Warning Mechanism in Education (*Mecanism de Avertizare
Timpurie în Educație*). Real-time data system identifying students at
risk of dropping out. See OECD 2025 ch. 4.

**Meditațiile** — Private tutoring outside school hours. Widespread in
Romania, especially around Evaluare Națională and Bacalaureat. Treated by
this brief as the informal connective tissue of the elite track (§5.4).

**ME / MEC** — *Ministerul Educației* (and previously *Ministerul
Educației și Cercetării*, when education and research are merged).
Different abbreviation used at different reform cycles for the same
ministerial role.

## N

**NEET** — Neither in Employment nor in Education or Training. Standard
Eurostat indicator; Romania has one of the EU's highest NEET rates,
particularly in rural areas.

**NRRP / PNRR** — National Recovery and Resilience Plan / *Planul
Național de Redresare și Reziliență*. The EU-funded post-COVID recovery
envelope. Romania's PNRR education component is approximately €3.6
billion through 2026 and funds the dual VET campuses, school
digitalisation, and PNRAS dropout-reduction programme.

## O

**OECD** — Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development.
Romania is in the accession process; the 2025 *Education and Skills in
Romania* review is part of that process.

**Olimpiodemie** — David's coinage in QX: literally "Olympic-epidemic",
critiquing the 500+ competitions the Ministry of Education runs
annually as a distortion of regular instruction. See §5.4.

## P

**PISA** — Programme for International Student Assessment. OECD's
triennial assessment of 15-year-olds in math, reading, science. Romania
first participated in 2002, continuously since 2006.

**PNRAS** — *Programul Național de Reducere a Abandonului Școlar* /
National Programme for Reducing School Dropout. PNRR-funded; in
>2,500 schools as of 2025. The OECD 2025 review endorses it.

**PNRR** — see **NRRP**.

**Preșcolar / Antepreșcolar** — Pre-school (ages 3-6) / under-3.
Antepreșcolar education is the layer of Romanian provision the OECD
2025 review most strongly recommends expanding.

## Q

**QX** — *Raportul QX* (David, May 2025). David's framing: "Q" for the
questions one must ask, "X" for the unknowns of the future.

## R

**RPL** — Recognition of Prior Learning. Mechanism for converting
informal / non-formal learning into formal qualification. Romania's RPL
system covers only vocational levels up to ISCED 3; the OECD recommends
extending to post-secondary and tertiary.

**România Educată** — *Educated Romania*. Strategic project initiated by
the Presidential Administration 2016, formalised 2021. The most
comprehensive strategic statement on Romanian education prior to the 2023
laws.

## S

**SAA** — Skills Assessment and Anticipation. Standard OECD framework
for matching education-system outputs to labour market demand.

**Salvați Copiii** — *Save the Children Romania*. The largest Romanian
children's-rights NGO. Publishes the *Abandonul școlar* annual reports
and runs *A doua șansă* operationally.

**SES** — Socio-Economic Status. PISA's composite background measure
(parental education, occupation, household assets).

**SEN** — Special Educational Needs. English term for what Romanian
documents call **CES**.

**SIIIR** — *Sistemul Informatic Integrat al Învățământului din România*.
The Ministry of Education's main administrative data system.

**SMEs** — Small and Medium-sized Enterprises. ~68% of Romanian private-
sector employment per OECD 2025.

**SSMR** — *Societatea de Științe Matematice din România* / Romanian
Mathematical Society. Co-organises the national mathematical olympiads
and IMO team selection.

**STEM / STEAM** — Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics (plus
Arts in the STEAM variant). QX strongly favours the STEAM framing.

## T

**TALIS** — OECD Teaching and Learning International Survey. Used for
the 93% "tied up by paperwork" principal-burden finding in OECD 2025.

**TEI** — Tertiary Education Institution. OECD's catch-all for
universities and other ISCED-5-and-above providers. Romania has ~83-87
TEIs (52 state, 35 private), with 29 concentrated in București and most
of the rest in Cluj-Napoca, Iași, and Timișoara.

**Tempo Online** — INS public-access database of Romanian statistics.

**TIMSS** — Trends in International Mathematics and Science Study.
Quadrennial international assessment of grade-4 and grade-8 students;
Romania participates.

**Titularizare** — The Romanian teacher-tenure-and-placement examination
and assignment system. QX proposes restructuring it; see §5.2 Layer 4.

**TSI** — Technical Support Instrument (formerly SRSS). The EU's funding
mechanism for member-state reform technical assistance. OECD's 2024
*Reforma învățământului preuniversitar* report on Romania was a TSI
project.

## U

**UEFISCDI** — *Unitatea Executivă pentru Finanțarea Învățământului
Superior, a Cercetării, Dezvoltării și Inovării* / Executive Agency for
Higher Education, Research, Development and Innovation Funding. Operates
HE and CDI competitive funding programmes.

**UNICEF** — UN Children's Fund. Romania country office publishes the
clearest data on out-of-school children and Roma educational exclusion.

**Universități** — Romanian universities. The large ones referenced in
this project: **UBB** (Babeș-Bolyai, Cluj), **UPB** (Politehnica
București), **UTCN** (Tehnică Cluj-Napoca), **UAIC** ("Alexandru Ioan
Cuza" Iași), **UPT** (Politehnica Timișoara), **ASE** (Academia de
Studii Economice, București).

## V

**VET** — Vocational Education and Training. Umbrella for both **IVET**
and **CVET**.

## W

**WB** — World Bank. Publisher of the 2023 *Romania Systematic Country
Diagnostic Update* cited by QX as a key reference.

**WVR** — World Vision Romania. Source for the *Bunăstarea copilului din
mediul rural* longitudinal report and the *Învățământul simultan*
evaluation.
