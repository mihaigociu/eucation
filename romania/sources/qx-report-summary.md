## 3. Executive verdict (David's own summary, §1 of QX)

1. **Research:** Romania (6th-largest EU population) produces too little knowledge, of which too little is of high quality, of which too little is of *Romanian conception* or transformed into innovation. System is fragmented, attracts no major international funding, vulnerable to brain drain. Human resources demotivated, often non-competitively employed.
2. **Higher education + LLL:** Fragmented national system; many universities developed atypically (incomplete-comprehensive, non-competitive HR); unclear/undifferentiated missions; obsolete internal organisation.
3. **Pre-university:** "Ineficient", with **poor competence quality, high dropout, elevated functional illiteracy — explicitly framed as a national security risk**.
4. **Political support** is needed for legislative change (mostly HGs, some laws), implementation of pending and new measures, and budget commitment.

## 4. Per-section claims

### 4.1 CDI / R&D&I (§3 of QX)

**System scale (2023-2024):**
- 87 universities; 43 INCDs (national R&D institutes); ~70 Romanian Academy units; private R&D units.
- ~32,000 researchers (~21,000 FTE).
- ~48,000 employees in R&D system (~35,000 FTE).

**Performance failures (with numbers David quotes):**

| Indicator | Romania | Comparison |
|---|---|---|
| Public R&D spending (% GDP, 2025) | ~0.5% | Legal target 1%; lowest in EU |
| Private R&D spending (% GDP) | ~0.3% | Legal target 1%; lowest in EU |
| Researchers per million inhabitants | <1,000 | EU avg doubled from ~2,000 to ~4,000 in 10 years; **Romania dropped from 1,300 → <1,000** |
| Nature index, natural sciences (2024) | 20 / 27 EU | |
| H-index, all sciences 1996-2023 | 17 / 27 EU | |
| Essential Science Indicators 2015-2025 | 18 / 27 EU | |
| Global Innovation Index 2024 | **Last in EU** | |
| Technological expertise (USNews) | 67 / 89 globally | |
| Horizon Europe budget attracted | 18 / 27 EU | |
| Horizon Europe participations | 16 / 27 EU | |
| Patent applications / GDP | 0.2% | EU avg 3.3% |
| Top publications (% of total) | 6.2% | EU avg 9.6% |

**Root causes David identifies (7):**
1. System fragmentation (architecture, coordination, sub-critical-mass units).
2. Organisational culture: universities historically built as teaching schools, not science institutions; Academy units too many and too small; INCDs expect base budget rather than competing for self-financing; mission confusion.
3. Insufficient, geographically uneven, demotivated human resources; weak excellence mindset; permanent contracts at low grades; system closed to outside (no head-hunting).
4. Poorly designed R&D infrastructure investments.
5. Chronic under-financing (0.5% / 1% gap).
6. Unpredictability of funding.
7. Inconsistent research ethics.

**Strengths:** "islands of excellence" from tradition and individual effort; high motivation derived from psychocultural attributes; competent Romanian scientific diaspora willing to engage.

**Reform proposals:** evaluate all R&D units → three performance classes (I strategic, II relevant, III restructuring); concentrate academic units; new R&D career system with tenure-track; budget toward 1% public + 1% private; new R&D law to replace the fragmented current framework.

### 4.2 Higher education + LLL (§4 of QX)

**System scale (2023-2024):**
- 87 institutions (52 public, 35 private).
- 544,623 students: 414,998 licență, 104,934 master, 21,630 doctorat, 3,061 postuniversitar.
- 52,355 staff (29,923 academic).

**Performance failures:**

| Indicator | Romania | Comparison |
|---|---|---|
| Adult tertiary attainment | **16%** | EU 30% — last in EU |
| Tertiary attainment 24-34 | 23-26% | EU 40-43% |
| First-year licență dropout | **>40%** (rises at doctoral level) | |
| LLL participation (adults) | 1% (2020) → 5.4% (2022) | EU avg 11.9%; RO 2030 target 17.4% |
| Adult digital skills (adequate) | 33% | EU 54% |
| Adult functional illiteracy — literacy | 20.1% (37.1% incl. risk level) | Intl 8.9% |
| Adult functional illiteracy — numeracy | 27.5% (38.4% incl. risk level) | Intl 8.6% |
| Universities in Shanghai ranking | **0** | Belgrade, Budapest, Ljubljana, Novi Sad, Szeged, Vilnius, Zagreb all present |

**David explicitly states:** *"The idea that we have too many students and graduates is a MYTH."*

**Root causes (6):**
1. Fragmentation; the historic universities (BUC, Cluj, Iași, Timișoara) were stripped of biomedical and research components by the **1948 communist reform** and never rebuilt as comprehensive institutions; post-1989 created many small universities with unclear missions. France pre-2000s had similar problem and consolidated.
2. Unclear/undifferentiated missions across the three academic functions (education / research / society).
3. Rigid internal organisation — bureaucratic constraint that masters can only be opened where a licență exists in the same field, doctorate only where master exists, etc.
4. Inconsistent academic ethics ("form without substance").
5. Closed structures — universities hire their own graduates rather than head-hunting best available; permanent contracts at low grades; non-incentivising junior salaries.
6. No culture of continuous institutional innovation (microcredentials, non-formal, LLL).
7. Fragmented authority over LLL between Education and Labour ministries.

**Reform proposals (David's own implementations marked):**
- **Differentiated missions** — three university types: (1) education; (2) education + research; (3) education + advanced research. Adopted in Law 199/2023 (per David's proposal as rector); operationalised by Ordinul 3027/2025.
- **Flexible programme organisation** — Ordin 3027/2025.
- **Academic concentrations** between universities and between universities and institutes — Ordin 3225/2025 (bottom-up).
- Reform ethics framework.
- Open recruitment + tenure-track for brain-gain.
- Universities take on microcredential certification + LLL.
- **National Programme to Reduce University Dropout** — launched 2025, linked to PNRR.

### 4.3 Pre-university (§5 of QX) — **the spine of our brief**

- 2,875,944 pupils — **lowest in a decade** (was 3,164,470 in 2014). Largest drop in gimnazial.
- 515,934 preșcolari · 877,540 primar · 710,054 gimnazial · 597,745 liceu · 92,065 profesional · 82,606 postliceal.
- Antepreșcolar: 31,814 — 95% urban, 95% public. Only 485 dedicated units.
- 215,614 teachers (139,474 urban); 300,121 total staff.
- 5,972 state schools with legal personality + 10,452 secondary units arondate.

**Root causes (4 buckets):**

1. **Unmodernised teaching process:**
   - Curriculum overloaded (too many subjects, hours, content)
   - Unattractive textbooks
   - Ineffective teaching methods (and ineffective homework — David cites Dolean & Lervag 2021)
   - Evaluation + "olympiad-style" contests don't cover all key competences
   - **Private tutoring (meditații)** "can interfere unduly with the school process"
   - SEN children not effectively integrated
   - High dropout (David quotes the figures above)

2. **Deficient school infrastructure** → bad learning context, multi-shift (sometimes 3 shifts) and simultaneous teaching.

3. **Uncoordinated educational ecosystem** — role confusion, conflicts, fragmented non-cumulative development.

4. **Transversal / external causes:**
   - Politicisation and ideologisation
   - Socioeconomic conditions
   - Bureaucracy
   - Violence and drug use

**Strengths David identifies:**
- Olympiad performance proves the system *can* work when the conditions are right.
- Parents of high-performing pupils play a major role.
- The current cycle structure (3-18 obligatory) is sound.

**Reform proposals (7 blocks):**

1. **Curricular reform** — slim plans-cadru toward EU averages; 25% of each subject's time for consolidation/remedial; redesigned textbooks combining classical + digital + gaming, with difficulty levels; modern personalised teaching incl. AI pilots (already started 2025); **regulate meditațiile** so they cannot interfere with school hours; generalise antepreșcolar; **liceu curriculum reform is 20+ years overdue** — plans-cadru already in public consultation for 2026/27.
2. **Evaluation reform** — Evaluările Naționale at grades II/IV/VI to cover all key competences; grade VIII evaluation expanded and selection stakes lowered (any liceu can decide up to 50% of seats by its own specialist exam); bacalaureat formula in Law 198/2023 kept.
3. **SEN reform** — proper psycho-medical diagnostic; reduce false positives/negatives; Nordic model.
4. **Dropout reform** — PNRAS program (PNRR-funded, currently running) to be made permanent national programme. Independent analyses say it works.
5. **Infrastructure reform** — 2025 budget funded the elimination of all outdoor toilets ("toaletele din curte") via local-authority projects; PNRR preserved digital + science lab equipment; **rural campuses where settlements are too far apart**; hybrid learning where consolidation isn't possible; antepreșcolar in disadvantaged communities as opportunity equaliser.
6. **Ecosystem reform** — most detailed block:
   - **Teacher salary**: start at average wage at debut, rise by tenure/grades (at least matched to healthcare); performance-linked top-up via individual career plan; salary tied to annual standards; 5-yearly comprehensive evaluation; failed evaluation → mandatory training, then re-eval; eight-hour day in school.
   - **Teacher hiring overhaul** — replace current titularizare with a national licensing exam (≥7 = licence for life); then individual school competitions for actual posts; tenure-track for fixed-term posts; permanent if school confirms viability.
   - **Pupils as co-creators** — feedback as quality indicator; scholarships separated cleanly into social (administered by primării, not schools) vs excellence; real psychological counselling, not paper compliance.
   - **Parents** — recognised collaborators; school as source of evidence-based parenting practice.
   - **Local authorities** — funding contributors based on local data.
   - **Mental health and well-being** as foundation.
7. **Transversal reforms:**
   - **"Politicul se oprește la poarta școlii"** — replace the multi-structure inspectorate (3+ institutions per județ) with a single "Direcție" merging inspectorate + casa corpului didactic + CJRAE; ARACIP to function like ARACIS (no județean offices); inspector general is political, specialist inspectors and school directors via competition.
   - Equal opportunity and "no one left behind".
   - **Olympiads** — currently 500+ contests/year is overload ("olimpiodemie"); rationalise to one ministry-funded olympiad per discipline + partner-organised others; do **not** run primary-level olympiads under the ministry.
   - Reduce bureaucracy (teachers shouldn't double as economists/IT staff); digitalise and use AI.
   - Zero tolerance for violence and drugs; anonymous reporting (implemented 2025); two new high school subjects "Stilul de viață sănătos" + "Autoreglarea psihologică".
   - Minority-language education as competitive advantage.
   - **Homeschooling** — accepted under strict frame: written request, initial cognitive + socio-emotional testing, annual reporting, school-umbrella, renewable every 3 years.

## 8. Gaps in QX — where we must go beyond it

David's report has known blindspots, partly because of its political audience and partly because of his domain emphases:

1. **Roma education** is treated only obliquely (one mention of "etnie" as a socioeconomic factor). **Use UNICEF, REF, Salvați Copiii for granular data.**
2. **Roma/poverty/migration cross-cutting drivers** are subsumed under "socioeconomic conditions" rather than analysed as distinct mechanisms. World Vision and Salvați Copiii are richer.
3. **The IT/tech success story is essentially absent.** The brief's pipeline-to-big-tech narrative (§5.5) cannot be sourced from QX.
4. **Specific top high schools (colegii naționale)** and Olympiad infrastructure are not named — David is abstract. We need Edupedu / school reports / Olympiad team rosters.
5. **Quantification of *meditații*** — David acknowledges them but does not estimate household spend or scale. Survey research (CEAE, INS household surveys) needed.
6. **Brain drain of HS/uni graduates to tech jobs abroad** is mentioned for researchers, not for the broader pipeline.
7. **Reform implementation evidence** — QX claims PNRAS "works according to independent analyses" but does not cite them. We should track them down.
8. **Author standpoint** — David writes both as analyst and as the *minister implementing the reforms he proposes*. The diagnosis is broadly defensible; the reform self-evaluation should be triangulated against critics (e.g., Edupedu, EFOR, opposition academics).
