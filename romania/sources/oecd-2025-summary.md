## 2. The OECD's three-pillar frame

Unlike QX's chapter-by-chapter cycle, OECD organises everything under three principles applied recursively at every level (ECEC, school, tertiary, skills):

1. **Quality of programmes and outcomes** — focus on functional literacy and lifelong-learning foundations.
2. **Equality of opportunity and access** — explicitly framed as "bridging the urban-rural divide".
3. **Good governance** — funding, autonomy/accountability balance, cross-ministerial coordination.

This framing should be mirrored in the brief's recommendations (§5.9 of the plan) so OECD policy messages map cleanly.

---

## 3. Executive verdict (OECD's own)

The 2023 legislation + PNRR money is "Romania's most ambitious attempt to modernise education since the post-democratic transition". The reforms come at a moment of opportunity (declining poverty, narrowing inequality), but stark divides remain. The challenge is **translating ambition into sustained outcomes**, especially closing equity gaps, with the urban-rural divide as the central frame.

The report deliberately reads as a friendly accession review — it acknowledges the David ministry by name, treats existing reforms charitably, and frames recommendations as *how to make the planned reforms succeed*. It is not a hostile diagnosis. **Read it for the data and policy benchmarks, not for political teeth.**

## 5. The 9 numbered policy messages

This is the report's actionable spine. Each maps to a chapter, and the report ties recommendations to specific OECD-country examples.

### Quality of programmes and outcomes
1. **Continue with the planned transformation of how teachers are trained and supported.** Embed mentorship + collaboration first in rural primary clusters and nurseries. Address the competitive teacher merit-pay scheme + high-stakes appraisal that block a peer-feedback culture. Examples: NZ, US resource frameworks.
2. **Strengthen leadership to support pedagogical improvements within settings and across school clusters.** Give principals direct responsibility for pedagogical leadership (currently fragmented across commissions). Formalise distributed leadership roles. Reduce admin burden — 93% of principals say paperwork bogs them down.
3. **Better align post-secondary education and adult learning with needs of learners + economy.** Expand short-cycle tertiary (ISCED 5 — currently absent), dual bachelor's, second-chance education. Two-thirds of vocational students currently leave with no clear post-secondary route.

### Equality of opportunity and access
4. **Target funding + digital programmes to expand quality education and training in disadvantaged rural and Roma communities.** Rethink network planning; targeted funding schemes (training levy for SMEs in lagging regions); flexible / hybrid provision; remote tertiary delivery using NRRP digital capacity.
5. **Break down participation barriers for vulnerable students** — financial, administrative, information. Raise parent awareness on ECEC. Streamline tertiary admissions. Expand recognition of prior learning (RPL) beyond level 3 to include ISCED 4+.
6. **Create a delivery plan to roll out dual VET in rural areas.** Cluster small rural IVET schools with larger providers; use the **29 regional dual campuses** (PNRR-funded) as integrated hubs. Provide pedagogical training for company trainers in small rural firms.

### Good governance
7. **Refocus funding to early childhood and primary education** to address spending gaps that cement disadvantage; improve efficiency across all levels. Tertiary currently gets ~2x ECEC and ~3x primary per student — directly inequitable. Use spending reviews and policy evaluations more systematically. Revise the HE performance-funding model toward more institutional development support.
8. **Fast-track modernisation of education governance** — strengthen institutional autonomy while maintaining accountability. Refocus County Directorates (formerly CSI) on support, not inspection. Streamline ARACIIP (preuniv) and ARACIS (univ) frameworks. Build TEI capacity for research, innovation, engagement.
9. **Create a more unified approach to policymaking** — supercharge cross-ministerial collaboration. Make the inter-sectoral committee for early education permanent (beyond PNRR), include Ministry of Finance. Establish a coordinating committee for VET / adult learning / occupational standards across Education + Labour ministries. Develop a single cross-sectoral skills strategy.

## 7. Where OECD and QX converge / diverge

### Convergence (mutually reinforcing — quote together)

- **Functional illiteracy + dropout as core failure** — QX's 25/36/46% pyramid lines up with OECD's "~40% below Level 2 in reading/science, ~50% in math".
- **Urban-rural divide as core equity issue** — QX gives enrolment splits; OECD gives the **largest rural-urban PISA gap in the world** and 27.5% vs 3.3% early-leaving split.
- **ECEC under-investment** — both flag the antepreșcolar / under-3 enrolment hole.
- **Dual VET expansion** + **29 PNRR campuses** — both endorse.
- **Differentiated university missions** — OECD endorses David's three-tier framework (Law 199/2023, Ordin 3027/2025).
- **Tertiary attainment is too low** — QX: 16% adults / 23-26% young; OECD: 19% / "well below OECD 41%". David's "we have too many graduates is a MYTH" matches OECD's view.
- **Governance fragmentation** — both flag Education/Labour split for adult learning + skills.

### Divergence (the OECD adds what QX misses)

| Area | QX | OECD | Note for brief |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Roma** | Briefly mentioned as "ethnie" | **Distinct section** with hard numbers: >80% of OOSC, 22% upper-sec completion, segregation | OECD is the primary source on Roma. |
| **Demand-side barriers** | Mentions socioeconomic conditions generally | Detailed treatment: financial, admin, information, parental awareness | OECD's Policy Message 5. |
| **Teacher mentorship + leadership** | Treats teacher reform as salary/career structure | Centres on **pedagogical leadership**, school clusters, distributed leadership | OECD's Policy Messages 1-2 — very specific. |
| **Funding allocation** | Argues for **overall increase** (toward 6% GDP target) | Argues for **reallocation away from tertiary toward ECEC + primary**; tertiary is regressive in current form | This is a real disagreement worth flagging in §5.9 of the plan. |
| **Quality-assurance approach** | Argues for ARACIP-like-ARACIS | Argues for **shift from compliance to enhancement / improvement support** | Different mode of accountability. |
| **Performance-related anxiety + growth mindset** | Not discussed | PISA data on RO students' psychology — growth mindset 43% vs OECD 58% | Useful for the brief if we use behavioural framing. |
| **Cross-ministerial coordination** | Treated as territorial dispute between MoE and MoLabour | Treated as **coordination opportunity** — endorses the inter-sectoral committee | Less adversarial framing. |
| **Specific country exemplars** | None | Chile (school leadership), Scotland (collaborative improvement), Ontario (peer collaboration), Ireland/Spain (mature-student admissions), France ("1000 first days"), UK (Sure Start), Portugal (network planning), Norway (adaptive QA), Austria/Germany (dual VET) | Source for §5.9 international benchmarking. |

### Divergence (where QX adds what OECD misses)

- The **"olimpiodemie"** critique — OECD doesn't discuss the volume of olympics and their distortion effect.
- **Meditațiile** (private tutoring) — OECD silent.
- The **1948 communist reform** as long-arc cause of fragmented universities — OECD doesn't explore.
- The **CDI (R&D&I) system** — entirely absent from OECD report; QX is sole source.
- The **politicisation of inspectorate** as urgent reform — OECD treats it as a structural change (CSI → County Directorates) without the David-style "politicul se oprește la poarta școlii" framing.
- **Homeschooling** — not in OECD.

## 9. Notable framings worth quoting

- *"While a minority of Romanian students reach levels of excellence comparable to their peers in other European and OECD countries, many more leave school without mastering the basic competencies needed to participate fully in society."* — succinct version of the paradox.
- The **stability of PISA below-Level-2 share since 2006** is the report's most damning quiet finding — implicit indictment of a decade and a half of reform.
- *"More than half of Romanians in rural areas remain at risk of poverty and social exclusion."* — useful one-liner.
- The **regressive funding tilt**: "public funding per student in tertiary education is almost double that of ECEC and nearly three times that of primary education" + "Romania has the highest child poverty rate in the EU". The combination is rhetorically powerful.
- The **largest rural-urban PISA gap of any country in PISA** — single most distinctive Romania-specific statistic in the report.
- *"Over 80% of out-of-school children and youth in Romania are Roma"* — the Roma-failure metric.
